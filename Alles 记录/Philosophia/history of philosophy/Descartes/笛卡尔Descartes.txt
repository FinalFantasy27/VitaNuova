
整个哲学就像一棵树。树根是形而上学，树干是物理学，从树干上长出的树枝是所有其它科学，可以归结为三门科学，即医学、力学和道德。我所说的“道德”是指最高、最完美的道德体系，它以其它科学的完整知识为前提，是智慧的终极层次。

——笛卡尔，第一哲学沉思录
[T]he whole of philosophy is like a tree. The roots are metaphysics, the trunk is physics, and the branches emerging from the trunk are all the other sciences, which may be reduced to three princi ple ones, namely medicine, mechanics, and morals. By “morals” I understand the highest and most perfect moral system, which presupposes complete knowledge of the other sciences, and is the ultimate level of wisdom.
Meditations on First Philosophy, “The Author’s Letter” (9B:14), of the French translation, in 1647, of Descartes’s ([1644] 1983) (which originally appeared in Latin).

笛卡尔所说的“形而上学”实际上是我们现在所说的“认识论”（知识的理论）和“形而上学”或“本体论”（对构成现实的基本事物类型以及它们之间关系的探究）的结合
What Descartes called ‘metaphysics’ is really a combination of what we now call ‘epistemology’ (theory of knowledge) and ‘metaphysics’ or ‘ontology’ (an inquiry into the fundamental types of things that make up reality, and the relations holding among them). (Soames, p. 51)
