A theory T is said to have Skolem functions if for every formula ϕ(x; y) there is a term t(y) such that whenever M ⊨ T, b ∈ M, and ϕ(M; b) is non-empty, then t(b) ∈ ϕ(M; b).

By the Tarski-Vaught criterion, having Skolem functions is enough to ensure that every substructure of a model is an elementary substructure.

Every structure can be expanded to have Skolem functions, by a process called skolemization.
