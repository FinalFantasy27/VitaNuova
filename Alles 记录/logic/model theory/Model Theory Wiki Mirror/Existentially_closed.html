A ℒ-structure M is existentially closed in a class 𝒦 of ℒ-structures, if for any ℒ-structure N ∈ 𝒦 with M ⊆ N we have that M is 1-elementary in N, i.e., for every existential ℒ-formula $\exists\overline{y}\phi(\overline{x};\overline{y})$ and every $\overline{a} \subseteq M$ we have that

$N \vDash \exists\overline{y}\phi(\overline{a};\overline{y})\leftrightarrow M \vDash \exists\overline{y}\phi(\overline{a};\overline{y}).$

Also for a ℒ-theory T we say that M is an existentially closed model of T, if M is existentially closed in the class of models of T. ::: ::: :::
